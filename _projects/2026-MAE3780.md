During the final 5 weeks of the Spring 2026 semester, I worked in a group of 3 to build a small robot controlled by an Arduino Uno to compete against other robots in cube collecting. My main focus of this project was to design a system to launch a rubber duck at a desired time. Given the budget and size constraints, I designed a plastic spoon launcher with a rubber band and a hobby servo to launch a duck forward about 2 feet in front of the robot. I made a platform in CAD that was laser cut with acrylic and attached above the Arduino, breadboard, and wiring with standoffs. The main strategy is for the servo arm to hold down the spoon with the duck loaded until the servo receives a signal to rotate allowing the spoon to rotate upward, launching the duck.

Throughout this project, I collaborated on the other aspects of the robot such as programming the Arduino to make the wheels rotate the correct amount to allow the robot to turn as we wanted. I also wired most of the circuit, primarily using H-bridges on a breadboard.
