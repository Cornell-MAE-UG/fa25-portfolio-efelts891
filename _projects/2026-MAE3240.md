Homework assignment #5 for MAE 3240 - Heat Transfer focused on exploring the effects of fin arrays for a heat sink. Problem #1 started with finding the temperature of a single fin with an adiabatic tip at the end and halfway through. I then was asked to find the fin efficiency and effectiveness in two ways and compared the accuracy of the methods. The last parts applied the concepts for one fin to a 5 x 5 fin array. 

![Schematic of Thermal System]({{ "/assets/images/heat_transfer.jpg" | relative_url }}){: .inline-image-l}
Through this assignment, I learned that the approximation method is quite close to the more complicated exact method for calculating fin efficiency. I also learned that using a fin in a heat sink is quite effective at reducing the temperature at the surface of the plate as much more heat is dissipated through the fin than the base. In the future, I can use this knowledge to design more effective cooling systems to protect sensitive electronics that generate a lot of heat.
